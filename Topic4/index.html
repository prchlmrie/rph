• Declaration of Philippine Independence

    Declared on June 12, 1898, this historic document marked the end of 333 years of Spanish colonization. It provides crucial insights into how revolutionaries perceived Spanish colonization and justified their fight for freedom.

  • Historical Significance

    This 2,000-word document summarized the revolution against Spain, the war of independence, and outlined the future of the new republic under Emilio Aguinaldo. The conspicuous absence of the Katipunan in the narrative indicates persisting factions in the revolutionary movement.

  • Justifications for Revolution

    The document cited numerous abuses, including those by the Civil Guards and the unlawful shooting of prisoners. It highlighted the unequal protection of law between Filipinos and Spaniards, and condemned the greed of the clergy, including friars and the Archbishop.

  • Looking Forward

    The document not only marked the end of Spanish rule but also foreshadowed American colonization. It remains an important historical text that reveals the revolutionaries' mentality and their analysis of the Philippines' situation at that time.
