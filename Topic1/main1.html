Diego Silang

Pangasinan

Diego Silang was born at Pangasinan, Aringay on December 16, 1730. His father was Pangasinese, while his mother was an Ilocano. He was batipzed on January 7, 1731.

Orphan

Diego Silang was an orphan who lived with the parish priest of Vigan. Cortes y Crisolo, the one who took care of Diego, teaches him the Spanish language. Soon, Diego has a good command of the Spanish language.

Messenger

Diego worked as a messenger who frequently traveled to Manila. Bright, passionate, and fluent in Spanish, he ferried correspondence from the Ilocos to Manila; journeys that gave him his first glimpse of colonial injustice and that planted the seed of rebellion.

Education

Diego received education under the friars.

Josefa Gabriela

Wife of Diego Silang. From Santa, Ilocos Sur. Married at the age of 27. Widow
