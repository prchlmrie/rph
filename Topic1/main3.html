Diego Silang's Letter to the Britain

Renato Constantino

Renato saw the first phase of Silang's revolt as Reformist (supporting or advancing gradual reform rather than abolition or revolution.)

When Spanish authorities refused to give in to his particular demands, SIlang started to take bolder actions.

Actions done by Diego Silang:

Expulsion

Silang declared the independence of Ilocandia, naming the state "Free Ilocos" and proclaiming Vigan its capiutal, and he expelled the alcalde and all the Spaniards from Vigan in 1762.

Abolishing

Silang abolished tributes, forced labor, and freed people from their obligation to serve the pricipales.

Fine

Imposed a 100 pesos fine from each priest.

Ultimatum

Upon receiving an ultimatum from the Spaniards, Silang turned to the British for Protection.

Shift in Diego Silang's Revolt

Protection

Silang intended to protect the Spanish Crown and the church from the British.

Proclamation

He proclaimed allegiance to the British and sought their protection.
