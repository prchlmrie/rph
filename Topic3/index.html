Historical Overview

Philippine Revolution

The Philippine Revolution signaled the end of the long Spanish Era. The revolution started in August 1896, upon the discovery of the Katipunan. The Katipunan was led by Andres Bonifacio.

The Secret Society

KKK

Kataastaasan, Kagalang-galang na Katipunan ng mga Anak ng Bayan, or Katipunan. Envisioned a united Filipino nation revolting against the Spaniards for the total independence of the country from Spanish.

Katipunan

Two Paths to Freedom

Ideologies

Katipunan sought revolt and total separation from Spain. Meanwhile, the Ilustrados pursued the Propaganda Movement, not envisioning total separation but demanding equal rights, representation, and protection from the abuses of the Friars.

Paths

Code of the Katipuneros

Kartilya

The Kartilya ng Katipunan embodied the values of KKK and serves as the code of Katipuneros. Written by Emilio Jacinto at age 18, it contained rules for personal conduct and treatment of fellow members. Original title: "Manga Aral Nang Katipunan ng mga Anak ng Bayan".

Historical Memoir

Mga Gunita

Emilio Aguinaldo's memoir "Mga Gunita ng Himagsikan" provides crucial insights into the revolution's leadership transition. Written from 1928 to 1940s, it details the conflicts between Magdalo and Magdiwang chapters, and his differences with Bonifacio.
